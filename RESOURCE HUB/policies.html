Policies

The Ministry of Education is guided by a core mission to provide quality, equitable, and inclusive education to all citizens, supporting national development through knowledge and skill-building. Its policies are shaped by a commitment to lifelong learning innovation, and ensuring no learner is left behind.

Ministry Of Education's Policies

  • Education for Everyone: The goal is to make sure every child in Guyana can go to school, no matter where they live or their background.
  • Better Curriculum: The Ministry keeps updating what students learn in school so it stays useful, modern, and helps prepare them for real life.
  • Literacy and Numeracy Improvement: Special emphasis on improving literacy and numeracy through targeted programs and early support.
  • Digital Transformation: Embracing digital learning through online resources, virtual classrooms, and e-books.
  • Inclusive Education: Creating an inclusive system that accommodates students with special needs.
  • Teacher Development: Focus on training and upgrading teachers' skills across all levels.
  • Student Welfare: Supporting attendance through feeding programs and resource distribution.
  • Quality Assurance: Regular evaluation of schools and educational programs.
  • Early Childhood: Strengthening nursery education as the educational foundation.
  • Assessment Reform: Aligning methods with learning outcomes for NGSA, CSEC, and CAPE.
